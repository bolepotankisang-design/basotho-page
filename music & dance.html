music & dance

moropa

The moropa is a goblet-shaped drum from Lesotho, also known as meropa or moropa drums. It was historically used in traditional Basotho music, sometimes including a jingle of bottle tops known as manyenenyene, and was an accompaniment to spiritual practices and initiation rites.

mamokhorong

Traditional musical instruments include lekolulo, a kind of flute played by herding boys; setolo-tolo, resembling an extended jaw harp played by men using their mouth; and the women's stringed thomo.

seliba

The lesiba is Lesotho's unique national musical instrument, a mouth-resonated bow that is a fusion of wind and string instrument properties.

ntlamu

Indlamu is a traditional dance originating from the Zulu tribe of South Africa, not Lesotho, though it is sometimes performed by the Matebele people who live in Lesotho and have ties to the Zulu culture.

maqekha

The maqekha is a special dance that forms part of the first rites of girls' initiation. The Basotho have a variety of musical instruments.

mokhibo

A dance performed by women, characterized by kneeling on the ground and rising and falling gently with the body, while sweeping the hands upwards. It often involves an informal choir, female dancers, clapping, and a steady rhythm.

mohobelo

This is a men's dance that demands significant energy and endurance. It is typically performed in the evenings for amusement and entertainment, often requiring men to participate for longer durations.

litolobonya

A unique form of music and dance that is performed exclusively by Basotho women and girls. It is a genre of song and dance that carries specific meanings related to women's experiences and realities

setolo-tolo

A mouth bow, often made with a hollow bamboo and horsehair string, played by men.

sekebeku

A wooden or metal jaw harp.

thomo

A gourd-resonated bow instrument played with a stick, primarily by women.

lenaka

Cow or goat horns played by traditional doctors or during initiation schools.
